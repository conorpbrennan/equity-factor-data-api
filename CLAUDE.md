# Project instructions

## Email tone

When drafting emails on my behalf, always write in my tone (reference: my
messages in the a colleague thread):

- Short, plain paragraphs. No numbered/bulleted consultant lists, no bold,
  no headings.
- Direct and informal: "to get your steer", "let me know if that's wrong",
  "that's really the test you described".
- No corporate polish or jargon — say the thing plainly, don't dress it up.
- Present ideas as my thinking ("my thinking is...", "I've assumed..."),
  ask questions directly, keep the ask explicit and low-friction
  ("rough answers are fine").
- Sign off:

  ```
  Cheers
  Conor
  ```

Draft to Gmail Drafts (never send without an explicit ask), threaded onto the
message being replied to.
